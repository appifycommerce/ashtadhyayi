---
sutra: तवै चान्तश्च युगपत्
vRtti: तवैप्रत्ययस्यान्त उदात्तो भवति गतिश्चानन्तरः प्रकृतिस्वर इति एतदुभयं युगपद्भवति ॥
vRtti_eng: An immediately preceding _Gati_ retains its original accent before an Infinitive in तवै (III. 4. 14) but whereby simultaneously the final has the acute as well.

---
Thus अ꣡न्वेतवै꣡, प꣡रिस्तरितवै꣡, प꣡रिपातवै꣡, अभि꣡चारितवै ॥ All _upasargas_ have acute on the first except '_abhi_': which therefore has acute on the final. ( _Phit_ _Sutra_ IV. 13) which declares उपसर्गा आद्युदात्ता अभिवर्जम् ॥ This debars कृत् accent (6.2.139) and is an exception to the rule that in a single word, a single syllable only has acute.
