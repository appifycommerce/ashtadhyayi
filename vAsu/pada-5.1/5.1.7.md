---
sutra: खलयवमाषतिलवृषब्रह्मणश्च
vRtti: खलादिभ्यो यत्प्रत्ययो भवति तस्मै हितमित्येतस्मिन्विषये । छस्यापवादः ॥
vRtti_eng: "The affix यत् comes in the sense of 'good for that,' after the word '_khala_,' _yava_,' '_masha_,' '_tila_,' '_vrisha_' and '_brahman_.'"
---
This debars छ. Thus खल्यम् 'suitable for threshing floor.' So also यव्यम्, माष्यम्, तिल्यम्, वृष्यम् and ब्रह्मण्यम् ॥

No secondary derivatives can, however, be formed of the following वृष्णे हितम्, ब्राह्मणेभ्यो हितम्; the full phrases must be used in these cases, for these words take neither छ nor यत् though वृष is वृषन् and ब्रह्मन् is = ब्राह्मण ॥

The word च in the _sutra_ indicates that the rule applies to words other than those enumerated. Thus रथाय हिता = रथ्या ॥
