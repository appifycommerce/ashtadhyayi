---
sutra: वाचो व्याहृतार्थायाम्
vRtti: व्याहृतः प्रकाशितोऽर्थो यस्यास्तस्यां वाचि वर्त्तमानाद्वाक्शब्दात्स्वार्थे ठक् प्रत्ययो भवति ॥
vRtti_eng: The affix ठक् comes after वाच् when it means the purport of the message spoken by another.
---
The word व्याहृत means 'declared, expressed'. That which was spoken formerly by another, and which is carried by a third party as a 'tiding' or 'news', is called व्याहृतार्थ ॥ Thus वाचिकं कथयति 'he relates the verbal message'. वाचिकं ऋद्धे ॥ Some say it applies to written message also.

Why do we say "when it means a verbal message ?" Observe, मधुरा वाक् देवदत्तस्य 'the speech of _Devadatta_ is sweet'.
