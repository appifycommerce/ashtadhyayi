---
sutra: उत्तरपदवृद्धौ सर्वं च
vRtti: उत्तरपदस्येत्यधिकृत्य या विहिता वृद्धिस्तद्वत्युत्तरपदे सर्वशब्दोदिक्शब्दाश्चान्तोदात्ता भवन्ति ॥
vRtti_eng: Words denoting direction and the word सर्व have acute on the final, before a word which takes _Vriddhi_ in the first syllable of the second term by (VII. 3. 12) and (VII. 3. 13).
---  
By the _sutra_ उत्तरपदस्य (VII. 3. 10). (VII. 3. 12), the _Vriddhi_ of the _Uttarapada_ is ordained when the _Taddhita_ affixes having ञ्, ण् or क् follow, the _Purvapada_ being सु, सर्व and अर्ध ॥ The word उत्तरपदवृद्धिः therefore, means that word which takes _Vriddhi_, under the rule relating to _uttarapada_, i. e. under rule (VII. 3. 12) and (VII. 3. 13). Thus पूर्वेपाञ्चालकः, अपरपांचालकः, सर्वपाञ्चालकः ॥ These are formed by वुञ् affix (IV. 2. 125). Why do we say "which takes _Vriddhi_ in the second term?" Had the word उत्तरपद not been used, then the _sutra_ would have run thus वृद्धौ सर्वं च, and would have applied to cases like सर्वमासः, सर्वकारकः where मासः and are कारकः are _Vriddha_ words not by virtue of (VII. 3. 12).
