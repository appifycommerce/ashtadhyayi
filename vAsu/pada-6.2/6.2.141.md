---
sutra: देवताद्वन्द्वे च
vRtti: देवतावाचिनां यो द्वन्द्वस्तत्र युगपदुभे पूर्वोत्तरपदे प्रकृतिस्वरे भवतः ॥
vRtti_eng: In a _Dvandva_ compound of names of Divinities, the both members retain their original accent.

---
Thus, इ꣡न्द्रासोमौ꣡, इ꣡न्द्राव꣡रुणौ, इ꣡न्द्राबृ꣡हस्पती ॥ The word इन्द्र has acute on the first (by _nipatana_), सोम is formed by मन् (_Unadi_ I. 140), and has acute on the first (VI. I. 197), वरुण is formed by उनन् (_Unadi_ III. 53) and by (VI. I. 197) has acute on the first. बृ꣡हस्प꣡ति has two acutes by (VI.2.140), and _Indra_-_Brihaspati_ has three acutes. Why do we say "names of divinities"? Observe प्लक्षन्यग्रोधौ ॥ Why do we say 'a _Dvandva_'? Observe अग्निष्टोमः ॥
