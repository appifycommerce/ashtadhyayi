---
sutra: सुखप्रिययोर्हिते
vRtti: सुख प्रिय इत्येतयोरुत्तरपदयोर्हितवाचिनि तत्पुरुषे समासे पूर्वपदं प्रकृतिस्वरं भवति ॥
vRtti_eng: The first member of a _Tatpurusha_ preserves its accent, when the second term is सुख or प्रिय, and the sense is 'to feel delight, or is good'.
---
Thus ग꣡मनसुखम् 'the pleasure of going'. So also व꣡चनसुखम्, व्याह꣡रणसुखम्, ग꣡मनप्रियम्, व꣡चनप्रियम्, व्याह꣡रणप्रियम् ॥ These are appositional compounds. All the above words are formed by ल्युट् affix and have acute on the syllable preceding the affix (VI. I. 193). The words _sukha_ and _priya_ have the sense of हित or 'well' 'good' 'beneficial', i. e. when the thing denoted by the first term is the cause of pleasure or delight. When this is not the sense we have परमसुख꣡म्, परमप्रिय꣡म् ॥
