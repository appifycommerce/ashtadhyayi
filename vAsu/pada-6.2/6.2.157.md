---
sutra: अच्कावशक्तौ
vRtti: अच् क इत्येवमन्तमशक्तौ गम्यमानायामुत्तरपदं नञः परमन्तोदात्तं भवति ॥
vRtti_eng: "A word formed with the _krit_ affix अच् and क, precelled by the particle नञ्, has acute on the final, when the meaning is 'not capable.'"
---  
Thus अपचः = य पक्तुं न शक्नोति, so also अजयः, अपठः, अविक्षिपः, अविलिखः (III. 1. 134) &c)). Why do we say when meaning 'not capable'? Observe अपचो दीक्षितः, अपचः परिव्राजकः ॥ A दीक्षित and a परिव्राजक do not cook their food, not because they are physically incapable of cooking, but because by the vows of their particular order they are prohibited from cooking.
