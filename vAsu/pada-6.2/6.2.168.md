---
sutra: नाव्ययदिक्शब्दगोमहत्स्थूलमुष्टिपृथुवत्सेभ्यः
vRtti: अव्यय दिक्शब्द गो महत् स्थूल मुष्टि पृथु वत्स इत्येतेभ्यः परं मुखं स्वाङ्गवाचि बहुव्रीहौ समासे नान्तोदात्तं भवति ॥
vRtti_eng: In a _Bahuvrihi_, the acute does not fall on such मुख denoting a real mouth, when it comes after an Indeclinable, and a name of a direction, or after गो, महत्, स्थूल, मुष्टि, पृथु and वत्स ॥

---
Thus: अव्ययः - उच्चै꣡र्मुखः, नीचै꣡र्मुखः ॥ The words उच्चैः and नीचैः are finally acute and retain their accent. दिक् - प्रा꣡ङ्मुखः, प्रत्य꣡ङ्मुखः ॥ The word प्राङ् has acute on the first by (6.2.52), and प्रत्यङ् is finally acute by (6.3.139). गो &c :- गो꣡मुखः, महा꣡मुखः, स्थूल꣡मुखः, मुष्टिमु꣡खः पृथु꣡मुखः and वत्स꣡मुखः ॥ In these the first members of the _Bahuvrihi_ preserve their respective accents under Rule (6.2.1) and in the case of compounds preceded by गो, मुष्टि and वत्स, the optional rule taught in the next _sutra_ is also superseded by anticipation, though the words may denote comparison.
