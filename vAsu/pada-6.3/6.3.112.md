---
sutra: सहिवहोरोदवर्णस्य
vRtti: सहि वहि इत्येतयोरवर्णस्योकार आदेशो भवति ढ्रलोपे ॥
vRtti_eng: "When ढ् or र् are elided, there is the substitution of ओ for the अ or आ of the verbs सह् and वह् ॥"
---
Thus सोढा, सोढुम्, सोढव्यम्, वोढा, वोढुम् and वोढव्यम् ॥ Why do we say of अ or आ? Observe ऊढः, ऊढवान् ॥ Why do we say अवर्णे which includes long आ also ? The rule will apply even when the short अ of सह and वह is changed to आ by _Vriddhi_: as उदवोढाम्, उदवोढम् ॥ Had merely अ been read into the _sutra_, instead of अवर्ण then coming after the त् of ओत्, it would have denoted only short अ (तादपि पर = तपरः (I. 1. 75)).
