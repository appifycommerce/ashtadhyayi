---
sutra: आनङ् ऋतो द्वन्द्वे
vRtti: ऋकारान्तानां विद्यायोनिसंबन्धवाचिनां यो द्वन्द्वस्तत्रोत्तरपदे पूर्वपदस्यानङादेशो भवति ॥
vRtti_eng: In a _Dvandva_ compound of words ending in short ऋ and expressing relationship through study or blood, the आनङ् is the substitute of the ऋ of the first member.

---
Thus होतापोतारौ, नेष्टोद्गातारौ, प्रशास्ताप्रतिहर्त्तारौ मातापितरौ, याताननान्दरौ ॥ The substitute is really आ, the न् is useful only to prevent the application of (1.1.51), by which a र comes after every simple vowel substitute of ऋ ॥

Why do we say 'both words ending in ऋ'? Observe पितृपितामहौ ॥ The _anuvritti_ of पुत्र is understood here, so that the ऋ of the first member is changed to आ before पुत्र also, though it does not end in ऋ. As पितापुत्रौ, मातापुत्रौ ॥
