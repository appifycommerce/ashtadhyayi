---
sutra: रुष्यमत्वरसंघुषास्वनाम्
vRtti: वेति वर्त्तते । रुषि अम त्वर संघुष आस्वन इत्येतेषां निष्ठायां वा इडागमो न भवति ॥
vRtti_eng: "The Participial affix may optionally get इट् augment, after रुष्, अम्; त्वर, संघुष्, and आस्वन् ॥"
---
As रुष्टः or रुषितः ॥ By (VII. 2. 48), the affixes after रुष् are optionally सेट्, and therefore by (VII. 2. 15), the _Nishtha_ after this verb would have been always _anit_; hence this optional rule. अभ्यान्तः or अभ्यमितः; तूर्णः or त्वरितः ॥ The त्वर् is exhibited in the _Dhatupatha_ as ञित्वरा (I. 812) i.e. with an indicatory long आ, and hence by (VII. 2. 16). would have been _anit_ always, this rule makes it optionally _anit_. So also, संघुष्टौ पादौ, or संघुषितौ पादौ, संघुष्टं or संघुषितं वाक्यमाह, संघुष्टौ or संघुषितौ दम्यौ ॥ घुष preceded by सम् will be optionally _anit_, even when having any sense other than that of 'proclaimed', as this subsequent _sutra_ supersedes (VII. 2. 23) so far. So also आस्वान्तः or आस्वनितौ देवदत्तः, आस्वान्तम् or आस्वनितं  मनः ॥ स्वन् when preceded by आ, though denoting 'mind,' is optionally _anit_, in spite of (VII. 2. 18), that rule being superseded so far by this subsequent rule.
