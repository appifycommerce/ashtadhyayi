---
sutra: उपदेशेऽत्वतः
vRtti: उपदेशे यो धातुरकारवान् तासौ निस्यानिट् तस्मात्तासाविवथलीडागमो न भवति ॥
vRtti_eng: After a root which possesses a short अ as its root-vowel in the original enunciation (the _Dhatupatha_), and after which the Periphrastic Future तास् is always devoid of the augment इट्, थल् the Personal ending of the Perfect, like तास्, does not get the augment इट् ॥

---
As Future पक्ता, Periphrastic पपक्थ, यष्टा, इयष्ट, शक्ता, शशक्थ ॥ Why do we say 'in the Original Enunciation'? Observe Future कर्ष्टा Periphrastic चकर्षिथ in which the अ of '_karsh_' is the result of _guna_ substitution. Why do we say 'having an अ'? Observe Future भेत्ता Periphrastic बिभेविथ, here the root-vowel is इ ॥ Why do we say "short अ"? Observe Future राद्धा Periphrastic रराधिथ from राध् the root-vowel being long आ ॥ The word तास्वत् is understood here also. Therefore we have जिघृक्षति, जिग्रहिथ ॥ Here the root ग्रह is _anit_ before सन् (VII.2.12) and not before तास् so it will not be _anit_ before थ ॥ The words नित्यम् अनिटः are also understood here. Therefore the rule does not apply to आनञ्जिथ (VII.4.71), (VII.4.72). For the root अञ्जू (VII. 21) takes optionally इट् before तास् (VII.2.44) as अञ्जिता and अङ्क्ता ॥
