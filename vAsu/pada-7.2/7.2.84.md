---
sutra: अष्टन आ विभक्तौ
vRtti: अष्टनो विभक्तौ परत आकारादेशो भवति ॥
vRtti_eng: आ is substituted for the final of अष्टन् before a case-ending.

---
Thus अष्टाभिः, अष्टाभ्यः, अष्टानाम्, अष्टासु ॥ Why 'before a case-ending'? Observe अष्टत्वम्, अष्टता ॥ The rule of this _sutra_ is an optional one, and we have in the alternative अष्टभिः, अष्टभ्यः ॥ The आ in the _sutra_ indicates the individual letter आ, and not आ belonging to the general class आ ॥ For the generic आ would include the nasalised आं also, and as the letter replaced (अन्) is a nasal, the substitute would have been also nasal आँ, but it is not so. See (VI.1.172) and (VII.1.22). This rule applies also when the word stands at the end of a compound, as प्रियाष्टानः or प्रियाष्टौः (VII.1.22). The word विभक्ति governs the subsequent _sutras_ upto (VII.2.114).
