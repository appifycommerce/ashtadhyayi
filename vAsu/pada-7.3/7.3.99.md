---
sutra: अड्गार्ग्यगालवयोः
vRtti: रुदादिभ्यः पञ्चभ्यः परस्य अपृक्तस्य सार्वधातुकस्याडागमो भवति गार्ग्यगालवयोर्मतेन ॥
vRtti_eng: According to the opinion of _Gargya_, and _Galava_, the augment अट् comes before a _Sarvadhatuka_ affix consisting of a single consonant, after the above five roots  रुद् &c.

---
As अरोदत्, अरोदः, अस्वपत्, अस्वपः, अश्वसत् अश्वसः, प्राणत् प्राणः, अजक्षत्, अजक्षः ॥ The names of _Gargya_ and _Galava_ are mentioned for honoris causa.
The mention of these names is not for the sake of "option" (विकल्पार्थम्). Because the very injunction about अट्, would make the ईट् of the preceding _sutra_ optional. The mention of more than one _Acharya_ in the _sutra_ is also for this very reason.
