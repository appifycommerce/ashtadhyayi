---
sutra: पदात्
vRtti: पदादित्ययमधिकार प्राक्कृत्सने च सुप्यगोत्रादावित्येतस्माद्यदित उर्द्धमनुक्रमिष्यामः पदादित्येवं तद्वेदितव्यम् ॥
vRtti_eng: "Upto (VIII. 1. 68), inclusive should always be supplied the phrase 'after a _pada_'."
---
Whatever we shall teach hereafter should be understood to apply to that which comes after a _pada_. Thus the _sutra_ (VIII. 1. 19), teaches "of an _amantrita_". The phrase पदात्, must be supplied to complete the sense. That is, "of a _pada_, which is in the Vocative case, and which comes after another _pada_, all vowels become _anudatta_". Thus पचसि दे॒॒व॒॒द॒॒त्त "Cookest thou, _O_ _Devadatta_". Why do we say, "after a _pada_"? Observe देवदत्त पचसि ॥ Here _Devadatta_ is not _anudatta_ (VI. 1. 198).
