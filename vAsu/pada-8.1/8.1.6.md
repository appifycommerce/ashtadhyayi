---
sutra: प्रसमुपोदः पादपूरणे
vRtti: प्र सम् उप उत् इत्येतेषां पादपूरणे द्वे भवतो द्विर्वचनेन चेत्पादः पूर्यते ॥
vRtti_eng: "प्र, सम्, उप and उत् are repeated, when by so doubling, the foot of a verse is completed."
---
As प्र प्रायमग्निभरतस्य शृण्वे (_Rig_ VII. 8. 4), संसमिद्युवते वृषन् ॥ (_Rig_ X. 191. 1), उपोप मे परामृश (_Rig_ I. 126. 7), किं नोदुदु हर्षसे दातवाउ (_Rig_ IV. 21. 9). Why do we say when 'a foot of a verse is completed there by'? Observe प्रदेवं देव्या धिया ॥ This rule applies only to the Vedic verses, for there alone the Preposition may be used separate from its verb. In the secular literature, this rule has no applicability, as Prepositions are never so used.
