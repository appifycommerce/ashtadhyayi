---
sutra: चिदिति चोपमार्थे प्रयुज्यमाने
vRtti: अनुदात्तमिति वर्त्तते चिदित्येतस्मिन्निपाते उपमार्थे प्रयुज्यमाने वाक्यस्य टेरनुदात्तः प्लुतो भवति ॥
vRtti_eng: "The end-vowel at the end of a sentence becomes _anudatta_ and _pluta_, when the particle चित् is employed, denoting comparison."
---
The word अनुदात्तं is understood here. This _sutra_ ordains _pluta_ as well and not merely accent.

Thus अग्निचिद् भाया॒॒३त् 'may he shine as fire'. So also राजचिद् भाया॒॒३त् 'may be shine as a King'.  

Why do we say 'when the sense is that of comparison'? Observe कथं चिद् आहुः ॥ Here चिद् has the force of 'littleness or difficulty'.

Why do we say प्रयुज्यमाने 'when expressly employed'? Observe अग्नि र्माणवको भायात् 'Let the boy shine like fire'. Here चित् is understood, hence the rule does not apply.
