---
sutra: लोपः शाकल्यस्य
vRtti: वकारयकारयोः पदान्तयोरवर्णपूर्वयोर्लोपो भवति शाकल्यस्याचार्यस्य मतेनाशि परतः ॥
vRtti_eng: व् and य् preceded by अ or आ and at the end of a _pada_, are elided before an अश् letter, according to the opinion of _Sakalya_.

---
As क आस्ते or कयास्ते, काक आस्ते or काकयास्ते, अस्मा उद्धर or अस्मायुद्धर, द्वावत्र or द्वा अत्र, असावादित्यः or असा आदित्यः ॥

The name of _Sakalya_ is used to make it an optional rule. Therefore, where there is not the lighter articulation of व् and य् by the last _sutra_, there also in the other alternative the fuller sounds of व् and य् are heard. Thus there are three forms, heavy व् and य्, light व् and य् and elision of व् and य् ॥

When व् and य् are preceded by ओ, then there is elision by the next _sutra_ compulsorily.
